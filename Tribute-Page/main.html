Jomo Kenyatta

Mzee Jomo Kenyatta (c. 1897–1978) — Nation-builder, independence leader and Kenya’s first Prime Minister and President.

About

Jomo Kenyatta was a central figure in Kenya’s struggle for independence from British colonial rule. He served as the nation's first Prime Minister (1963–1964) and President (1964–1978). A farmer, politician and statesman, his leadership shaped early post-colonial Kenya.

Born in the Kikuyu community, Kenyatta studied abroad and returned to spearhead political organization, negotiation and nation-building. His influence was both celebrated and debated; this page provides a factual, respectful tribute to his role in Kenyan history.

Quick Facts
  • Full name: Kamau wa Muigai (commonly Jomo Kenyatta)
  • Born: c. 1897
  • Died: August 22, 1978
  • Known for: Independence leadership, first President of Kenya

Timeline

Selected milestones in Kenyatta's life.

c. 1897 — Early life

Born in the Mount Kenya region; original name Kamau. Early years spent in traditional Kikuyu society and later worked in Nairobi and London.

1920s–1930s — Studies & activism

Traveled and studied in Europe; engaged with Pan-African and anti-colonial movements, began writing and speaking on rights for Africans.

1940s — Political leadership

Returned to Kenya and took an increasingly prominent role in political organization and negotiation with colonial authorities.

1963 — Independence

Kenya gained independence on December 12, 1963. Kenyatta became Prime Minister and later President in 1964.

1978 — Passing

Jomo Kenyatta died on August 22, 1978, leaving a complex but lasting legacy in Kenya's political and social history.
